Why Nikola Jokic Deserves the 2024 MVP

Nikola Jokic continues to elevate the standard for what an NBA center can do. His vision, touch, passing, and leadership make him a once-in-a-generation talent. In the 2023-24 season, Jokic averaged:

  • 26.4 PPG (Points Per Game)
  • 12.4 RPG (Rebounds Per Game)
  • 9.0 APG (Assists Per Game)
  • 61% FG, 35% 3PT, 82% FT

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander, on the other hand, had a breakout year leading the Oklahoma City Thunder. He averaged:

  • 30.1 PPG
  • 5.5 RPG
  • 6.2 APG
  • 54% FG, 36% 3PT, 87% FT

Who Had More Impact?

While SGA posted impressive scoring numbers and led OKC to a top-three seed in the West, Jokic had a more complete impact on every aspect of the game. He’s the engine of the Denver Nuggets' offense, responsible for orchestrating plays, creating spacing, and defending at a high level.

Advanced metrics like Player Efficiency Rating (PER), Win Shares, and Box Plus/Minus consistently rank Jokic as the most valuable player in the league — not just this season, but over the past four seasons.

Why Jokic Should Win MVP

Jokic is not just putting up stats — he's winning games. The Nuggets have one of the best records in the league, and Jokic remains the core of everything they do. Unlike SGA who plays a traditional scoring guard role, Jokic plays like a point guard in a center’s body. He influences the game from all angles: offense, defense, tempo, leadership.

If the MVP is truly about being the most *valuable* player to your team, then no one fits that definition better than Nikola Jokic.
